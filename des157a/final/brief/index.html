Project Summary

For my final project, I will be creating a new, simple, and fun game! I will not be updating Studio 3, Game On!, but creating a new game from scratch. This game is designed to help increase focus, improve reflexes, and help coordinate between your left and right brain!

Project Goals

The goal of this project is to train user reflexes while also having fun! The idea of the game is simple: there will be two boxes that show up on the screen and the text will randomly show up on either side and will randomly be written as 'left' or 'right'. The challenge is that the user has to click on the box that is written. There will be a total timer countdown, and the user has to get as many correct as possible in the given time! From what I have seen online, most of the time there are just survey-styled 'games', that let the user know if you're more "right" or "left" oriented, but no game to actually help the user get in sync. My game is completely different in terms of concept from the two comparison projects, however, the user interactions will be similar to what we have seen on both these sites. I hope you have fun!

Design Strategies

The entire game site will be designed keeping in mind my overall project theme. Considering this, my color scheme will have a dark background like #242424 . The buttons will contrast to the background and be colorful, similar to my game on player 1 and 2 color schemes. The buttons will be symmetrically placed in the middle of the page, taking up a majority section since that is the major element of the game. The text inside the buttons will be centrally placed and of white color to make it easily noticeable to the user. A timer and a score counter will be placed above the buttons. The typography will be simple and common, like Helvetica, since the speed will be key here and the user shouldn't spend time trying to decipher what is written on the screen.

Interaction Strategies

As explained, the game is pretty simple. The game mostly revolves around clicking two buttons. The buttons on pressing will give a pressed effect along with an audio clip, to add to the user experience. A timer will also be placed in the top right with changing color to indicate the time remaining because the user's focus will mostly be on the buttons. Changing to a red color as time runs short will be easier for the user to tell time is running out without changing focus. Similarly, a score counter will also be on the screen that changes color in the opposite direction, so that the user gets positive feedback. The overall game design is intended to be sleek, something like a mobile game experience found online.

Experience Goals

This game is geared towards getting your left and right brain more in sync, the right will want to click on the pretty colors while the left will try and follow the written text! From scientific analysis, the left brain deals with thinking in words, linear thinking, and factual analysis. The right brain deals with things like visualization, imagination, intuition, and rhythm. This exercise helps the user to coordinate between the two, while also training their reflexes to click on the correct button. I hope that after playing the game a couple of times, their motor control increases over their reflexes, and they have had fun!
